Stucco Repair & Concrete Restoration in South Florida

South Florida's intense sun, driving rain, and salt-laden air create a uniquely challenging environment for exterior stucco finishes. Thermal expansion and contraction from extreme temperature swings cause hairline cracks that gradually widen, allowing moisture to penetrate beneath the stucco surface. Once water gets behind the finish, it can cause delamination, rot in underlying sheathing, and mold growth within wall cavities.

EIFS (Exterior Insulation and Finish Systems) present additional concerns in South Florida's climate. WhileEIFS provides excellent insulation, improper installation or failed sealants at windows, doors, and transitions can trap moisture behind the system. This hidden water damage often goes undetected until it causes significant structural problems. UV exposure degrades EIFS finish coats over time, leading to fading, chalking, and surface erosion that compromises both appearance and protective function.

Ignoring stucco damage leads to escalating repair costs. What begins as a simple crack repair can grow into full wall system replacement if moisture infiltration continues unchecked. Property managers and building associations that address stucco issues early avoid the higher expense of emergency repairs and prevent damage to interior spaces and tenant improvements.

Stucco damage often accompanies concrete deterioration on the same building, as both materials face the same environmental stresses. A comprehensive exterior restoration that addresses both stucco and concrete simultaneously delivers better value and more consistent protection. After stucco repairs are complete, applying professional painting with elastomeric coatings adds an additional moisture barrier and extends the life of the stucco finish.

Our Stucco Repair Process

1. Damage Assessment

Our team inspects the full extent of stucco damage including crack patterns, areas of delamination, and moisture conditions beneath the surface. We identify the root causes of deterioration to prevent recurrence after repairs are completed.

2. Removal & Preparation

Damaged stucco is carefully removed down to the substrate, exposing the underlying wall system for inspection. The substrate is cleaned, repaired as needed, and prepared to receive new stucco layers with proper adhesion.

3. Stucco Application

New stucco layers are applied according to industry standards, with embedded mesh reinforcement where required for crack resistance. Each coat is applied to proper thickness and allowed adequate curing time before the next layer.

4. Finishing & Curing

Final textures are matched to the surrounding surfaces for a seamless appearance. After application, the stucco is properly cured and inspected to verify adhesion, texture consistency, and long-term durability.
